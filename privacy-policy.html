Legal & Compliance Information

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 11th June 2026

Your privacy is critically important to us. At Millard Agingu & Company Advocates, we have a few fundamental principles:

  • We are thoughtful about the personal information we ask you to provide and the personal information that we collect about you through the operation of our services.
  • We store personal information for only as long as we have a reason to keep it.
  • We help protect you from overreaching government demands for your personal information.
  • We aim for full transparency on how we gather, use, and share your personal information.

Below is our Privacy Policy, which incorporates and clarifies these principles.

1. Who We Are and What This Policy Covers

We are a law firm based in Nairobi Kenya providing a variety of legal solutions to clients both within and outside Kenya. Our mission is to provide strategic legal advice tailored to our client needs in both business and personal matters. We endeavor to provide solutions that are not only geared towards compliance but also make commercial sense.

This Privacy Policy applies to information that we collect about you when you use:

This Privacy Policy applies to personal information that we collect, use, store, disclose, or otherwise process in connection with:

  • Our website and online platforms;
  • Our legal services and professional engagements;
  • Communications with prospective clients, existing clients, business partners, service providers, and other third parties;
  • Consultations, enquiries, meetings, and correspondence conducted in person, by telephone, email, messaging applications, video conferencing platforms, or other communication channels;
  • Events, seminars, publications, newsletters, legal updates, and other professional activities organised or provided by the Firm; and
  • Any other interactions through which we collect personal information in the course of operating our legal practice.

This Privacy Policy should be read together with any engagement letters, terms of business, retention notices, or other privacy notices that may be provided in connection with specific legal services.

Throughout this Privacy Policy we’ll refer to our website, legal services, and other products and services collectively as “Services.”

Below we explain how we collect, use, and share information about you, along with the choices that you have with respect to that information.

2. Information We Collect

We only collect information about you if we have a reason to do so — for example, to provide our Services, to communicate with you, or to make our Services better.

We collect this information from two sources: if and when you provide information to us, and automatically through operating our Services. We do not collect information from outside sources. Let’s go over the information that we collect.

Information You Provide to Us

It’s probably no surprise that we collect information that you provide to us directly. Here are some examples:

  • Contact details: During the ordinary course of business or through our website contact form you may provide Name, Email address, Telephone number and information contained in enquiries or messages submitted through the contact form.
  • Payment Information: For example if we act on your behalf on a particular transaction we may require your financial accounts details of where you may receive or send money related to the transaction. This can include information connected to digital payment systems.
  • Job applicant information: If you apply for a job with us, you may provide us with information like your name, contact information, resume or CV, professional or personal references, similar professional and employment-related data, and work authorization verification as part of the application process. We may also collect additional information about you during the process such as your background.

Information We Collect Automatically

We also collect some information automatically.

We collect the following from the use of our website:

  • IP address
  • Browser type
  • Device information
  • Website usage data
  • Analytics information

Transactional information: When you make a payment through our payment channels, we collect information about the transaction, such as the purpose of the transaction, purchase price, and the date and location of the transaction. This includes when you pay for something related to a service that we are acting on your behalf to a third party.

3. How and Why We Use Personal Information

Purposes for Using Information

We may use personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide legal services and fulfil our professional obligations to clients;
  • To assess, establish, and manage client relationships and engagements;
  • To respond to enquiries, consultation requests, and other communications;
  • To communicate with prospective clients, existing clients, business partners, service providers, regulators, and other relevant parties;
  • To conduct client due diligence, conflict checks, identity verification, and other onboarding procedures where required;
  • To prepare, review, negotiate, and administer legal documents, transactions, and legal matters;
  • To manage billing, invoicing, payments, and financial administration;
  • To comply with legal, regulatory, professional, ethical, and risk management obligations;
  • To protect the rights, property, safety, and legitimate interests of the Firm, our clients, and third parties;
  • To detect, prevent, investigate, and respond to fraud, security incidents, unlawful activities, or misuse of our Services;
  • To maintain records and documentation relating to our legal practice and professional engagements;
  • To improve our website, Services, client experience, and operational efficiency;
  • To ensure quality, maintain safety, and improve our Services;
  • To send legal updates, publications, invitations, newsletters, or other information where permitted by law or with your consent; and
  • For any other purpose that is reasonably related to the provision of our Services or otherwise permitted or required by law.

Legal Basis for Processing Personal Information

We process personal information only where we have a lawful basis to do so under applicable data protection laws, including the Data Protection Act, 2019 of Kenya and, where applicable, the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR").

Depending on the circumstances, we may process personal information on one or more of the following legal bases:

i. Consent

We may process personal information where an individual has freely given their consent for a specific purpose. Where processing is based on consent, such consent may be withdrawn at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions and reasonable notice.

ii. Performance of a Contract

We may process personal information where such processing is necessary to enter into, perform, administer, or fulfil a contract or professional engagement, including the provision of legal services requested by a client.

iii. Compliance with Legal Obligations

We may process personal information where necessary to comply with applicable laws, regulations, court orders, professional obligations, regulatory requirements, anti-money laundering obligations, reporting requirements, or other legal duties to which the Firm is subject.

iv. Legitimate Interests

We may process personal information where such processing is necessary for the legitimate interests pursued by the Firm or a third party, provided that those interests are not overridden by the rights, freedoms, or interests of the data subject.

Such legitimate interests may include:

  • Providing and improving our Services;
  • Managing client relationships and communications;
  • Conducting conflict checks and risk assessments;
  • Protecting the security and integrity of our systems, website, and operations;
  • Preventing fraud, abuse, and other unlawful activities;
  • Defending, establishing, or exercising legal claims; and
  • Managing and operating our legal practice efficiently and effectively.

v. Protection of Vital Interests

In limited circumstances, we may process personal information where necessary to protect the vital interests of an individual or another person.

vi. Public Interest or Exercise of Legal Functions

Where applicable, we may process personal information where such processing is necessary for reasons of public interest, the administration of justice, the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims, or the provision of legal services in accordance with applicable law.

Where we process personal information, we will take reasonable steps to ensure that such processing is lawful, fair, transparent, and limited to what is necessary for the relevant purpose.

How We Share Personal Information

We do not sell personal information. We share personal information only where necessary, proportionate, and subject to appropriate confidentiality and data protection safeguards.

We may disclose or share personal information in the following circumstances:

Service Providers and Professional Advisors

We may share personal information with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our website, managing communications, or delivering our legal services. This may include providers of:

  • Website hosting and infrastructure services;
  • Secure form submission and enquiry processing tools;
  • Email, messaging, and communication systems;
  • Document management and cloud storage services;
  • IT support, security, and maintenance services; and
  • Professional advisors such as accountants, auditors, insurers, or consultants.

These service providers are only permitted to process personal information on our instructions and are required to handle such information securely and confidentially.

Legal and Regulatory Disclosures

We may disclose personal information where required to do so by applicable law, regulation, court order, or professional obligation. This may include compliance with:

  • Judicial or court processes;
  • Regulatory or supervisory authorities;
  • Anti-money laundering and know-your-client obligations; or
  • Other legal or statutory requirements applicable to the Firm.

Professional and Legal Services Context

Where we are engaged to provide legal services, we may share personal information as necessary to act on behalf of a client. This may include disclosure to courts, tribunals, counterparties, government authorities, or other persons involved in a legal matter, strictly in accordance with the instructions of our client and applicable law.

Business Operations and Internal Use

We may share personal information internally within the Firm on a need-to-know basis for administrative, operational, compliance, and service delivery purposes.

Protection of Rights and Interests

We may disclose personal information where we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to:

  • Protect and defend the rights, property, or safety of the Firm, our clients, or others;
  • Detect, prevent, or respond to fraud, security incidents, or misuse of our Services; or
  • Establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

Corporate Transactions

In the unlikely event of a merger, restructuring, acquisition, or transfer of all or part of our practice, personal information may be transferred as part of the business assets, subject to continued protection in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

With Consent

We may share personal information where an individual has given us consent or directed us to do so.

Aggregated or De-identified Information

We may use or share aggregated, anonymised, or de-identified information that cannot reasonably be used to identify any individual. This may be used for internal analysis, service improvement, or reporting purposes.

4. How Long We Keep Personal Information

We retain personal information only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including for the provision of legal services, compliance with legal and regulatory obligations, and the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.

The retention period may vary depending on the nature of the information and the context in which it was collected. In determining appropriate retention periods, we take into account:

  • The duration of any professional or client relationship;
  • Ongoing or potential legal matters or disputes;
  • Statutory, regulatory, and professional obligations applicable to legal practitioners in Kenya;
  • Applicable limitation periods for legal claims; and
  • The need to maintain appropriate records for risk management, audit, and compliance purposes.

Where we act for a client, we may retain relevant personal information and client files for a reasonable period after the conclusion of the matter, in accordance with applicable law, professional duties, and internal record-keeping policies.

Where personal information is no longer required, we will take reasonable steps to securely delete, destroy, anonymise, or de-identify it in accordance with applicable law.

In some cases, we may be required to retain information for longer periods where required by law, court order, regulatory obligation, or professional standards, or where it is reasonably necessary for the protection of our legal interests.

5. Data Security

We take the security and confidentiality of personal information seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure.

These measures are designed to reflect the sensitivity of the information we handle in the course of providing legal services.

Depending on the nature of the information and the systems used, these measures may include:

  • Access controls restricting personal information to authorised personnel only;
  • Confidentiality obligations imposed on all staff and, where applicable, third-party service providers;
  • Use of secure systems and password-protected platforms for the storage and processing of data;
  • Secure communication channels for client correspondence where appropriate;
  • Regular review of internal processes and data handling practices; and
  • Reasonable safeguards to protect against unauthorised access or cyber threats.

While we take reasonable steps to protect personal information, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. Accordingly, we cannot guarantee absolute security of information transmitted to or from our website or through electronic communications.

Where we engage third-party service providers, we take reasonable steps to ensure that they implement appropriate security measures consistent with applicable data protection laws.

Clients and users are also encouraged to take reasonable steps to protect their own information, including safeguarding passwords and avoiding the transmission of sensitive information through insecure channels where possible.

6. International Transfers of Personal Information

As part of operating our website and delivering legal services, personal information may be transferred to, stored in, or accessed from jurisdictions outside Kenya.

This may occur where we use third-party service providers, cloud-based infrastructure, communication tools, or professional systems that operate internationally or rely on global data hosting networks.

Where such international transfers take place, we take reasonable steps to ensure that personal information is protected in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the Data Protection Act, 2019 of Kenya and, where applicable, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

These safeguards may include contractual obligations requiring service providers to maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures and to process personal information only in accordance with our instructions and applicable law.

By interacting with our website or engaging our services, you acknowledge that your personal information may be transferred and processed outside Kenya in accordance with this Privacy Policy and applicable legal requirements.

We will not transfer personal information internationally unless appropriate safeguards are in place to ensure an adequate level of protection.

7. Your Rights as a Data Subject

Depending on your location and the applicable data protection laws, including the Data Protection Act, 2019 of Kenya and, where relevant, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you may have certain rights in relation to your personal information.

These rights are not absolute and may be subject to legal, professional, or regulatory limitations, particularly where we are required to retain or process information in connection with legal services, court proceedings, or compliance obligations.

Right of Access

You may request confirmation as to whether we process your personal information and, where applicable, request access to that information together with details of how it is being used.

Right to Rectification

You may request correction or updating of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal information.

Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

You may request deletion of your personal information where there is no lawful reason for us to continue processing it. This right may be limited where we are required to retain information for legal, regulatory, professional, or legitimate business purposes.

Right to Restriction of Processing

You may request that we restrict the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances, for example while accuracy or lawful basis is being verified.

Right to Object

You may object to the processing of your personal information where such processing is based on legitimate interests or where it is used for direct marketing purposes.

Right to Data Portability (GDPR only, where applicable)

Where the GDPR applies, you may request to receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and request that it be transferred to another data controller where technically feasible.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner in Kenya, or with a relevant supervisory authority within the European Economic Area where the GDPR applies.

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of your rights, you may contact us using the details provided in this Privacy Policy.

We will respond to requests within the timeframes required under applicable law and may decline requests where lawful grounds exist for doing so.

When you contact us about one of your rights under this section, we’ll need to verify that you are the right person before we disclose or delete anything. You can also designate an authorized agent to make a request on your behalf by giving us written authorization. We may still require you to verify your identity with us.

Appeals Process for Rights Requests Denials

In some circumstances we may deny your request to exercise one of these rights. For example, if we cannot verify that you are the owner of the data you are requesting about we may deny your request to access the personal information associated with the individual data subject. As another example, if we are legally required to maintain a copy of your personal information, we may deny your request to delete your personal information.

In the event that we deny your request, we will communicate this fact to you in writing. You may appeal our decision by responding in writing to our denial email and stating that you would like to appeal within fourteen days of notice of the decision. All appeals will be reviewed by an internal expert who was not involved in your original request. In the event that your appeal is also denied this information will be communicated to you in writing.

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to ensure proper functionality, improve user experience, and understand how visitors interact with our website.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the website function efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Types of cookies we may use

We may use the following categories of cookies:

Strictly Necessary Cookies:

These cookies are required for the basic operation of our website, such as page navigation and security features.

Analytics Cookies:

We may use analytics tools, such as Google Analytics, to collect aggregated and anonymised information about how visitors use our website. This may include information such as pages visited, time spent on the site, device type, browser type, and general geographic region. This information helps us improve website performance and user experience.

Functionality Cookies:

These cookies may remember your preferences or inputs to improve usability when you return to our website.

Contact Forms and Third-Party Processing

When you submit an enquiry through our website, the information you provide is transmitted securely through third-party service providers that assist us in processing and delivering communications to the Firm. These providers act only on our instructions and are contractually required to handle your data securely and in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

Managing Cookies

Most browsers allow you to control or disable cookies through your browser settings. If you choose to disable cookies, some parts of the website may not function properly.
