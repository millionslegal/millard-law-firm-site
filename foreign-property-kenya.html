Overview of Property Ownership in Kenya

Property ownership in Kenya is governed by constitutional and land legislation that distinguishes between freehold and leasehold tenure systems. Foreigners are subject to specific restrictions regarding land ownership.

Can Foreigners Own Land in Kenya?

Foreign nationals are generally restricted from owning freehold land in Kenya. Ownership is typically limited to leasehold interests, usually for a term not exceeding 99 years.

  • Freehold land ownership is reserved for Kenyan citizens
  • Foreigners may hold leasehold property (commonly 33–99 years)
  • Leaseholds are renewable subject to government approval

Agricultural and Controlled Land

Agricultural land is subject to additional restrictions. In many cases, foreign ownership is prohibited unless special approval is granted, including in rare circumstances through presidential exemption or structured corporate arrangements compliant with law.

Corporate Structures and Nominee Arrangements

Foreign investors sometimes consider using local companies or nominee arrangements to acquire property. However, improper structuring may expose investors to legal risk, invalid transfers, or regulatory challenges.

Sectional Properties (Apartments & Units)

Foreigners may acquire sectional units such as apartments, provided the underlying title is leasehold. This structure is increasingly used in urban developments and high-value real estate projects.
