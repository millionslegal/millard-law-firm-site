Overview of Data Protection in Kenya

Kenya’s data protection regime is governed by the Data Protection Act, 2019. It establishes obligations for organizations that collect, process, store, or transfer personal data. The framework aligns closely with international standards such as the GDPR.

Why This Matters for Foreign Companies

International businesses operating in Kenya must comply with both local regulations and cross-border data transfer requirements. Non-compliance can lead to regulatory penalties, reputational risk, and operational disruption.

Key Compliance Risks

  • Unlawful processing of personal data
  • Improper cross-border data transfers
  • Lack of privacy policies aligned with Kenyan law
  • Weak internal data governance systems
